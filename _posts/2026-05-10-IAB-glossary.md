This note summarizes common ad-tech and media-buying concepts in a compact, interview-friendly way.

## 1. Big Picture

Most advertising terms can be organized into a simple pipeline:

```text
Advertiser / Brand
        ↓
Audience Data
        ↓
Buying / Trading
        ↓
Media / Publisher
        ↓
Ad Experience
        ↓
Measurement
````

In other words, an advertiser first defines the target audience, activates data, buys inventory, serves creative, and then measures whether the campaign reached the right people and created business value.

## 2. One-Page Summary

**Targeting starts with audience definition.**
Brands define verticals, demographics, and behavioral signals such as clickstream or online behavior.

**Data and platforms connect the buyer to inventory.**
A Data Management Platform, or DMP, helps create audience segments. A Demand-Side Platform, or DSP, helps advertisers and agencies buy ad impressions from publishers and exchanges.

**Buying can be direct or automated.**
Direct buying uses RFPs, insertion orders, rate cards, preferred rates, and upfront commitments. Automated buying uses programmatic buying and real-time bidding.

**Channels define where ads appear.**
Ads may appear on CTV, OTT, DOOH, websites, ad networks, splash pages, rich media placements, or video players using standards such as VAST and VPAID.

**Measurement asks two questions.**
Did the target audience see the ad? Did the campaign create business value? Common metrics include GRP, TRP, OTP, VCR, cross-screen measurement, and RODI.

|   8 | **Data Management Platform (DMP)**              | Platform used to collect, organize, and activate audience data; often used to create target audience segments.          |
|   9 | **Demand-Side Platform (DSP)**                  | Buyer-side platform for advertisers or agencies to buy ad inventory across exchanges and publishers.                    |
|  10 | **Dynamic creative**                            | Creative that changes automatically based on audience, context, product, location, or other signals.                    |
|  11 | **Earned media**                                | Exposure gained through sharing, PR, word of mouth, social reposts, and fans rather than directly purchased media.      |

|  14 | **Interstitial**                                | Full-screen or transition ad shown between content states, pages, screens, or app actions.                              |
|  15 | **Insertion Order (IO)**                        | Contract or order document between buyer and seller specifying campaign details, dates, pricing, placements, and terms. |

|  20 | **POE: Paid, Owned, Earned media**              | Media classification: paid ads, owned channels, and earned exposure.                                                    |
|  21 | **Point of awareness**                          | Campaign stage or goal focused on making users aware of a brand, product, or message.                                   |
|  22 | **Point of sale**                               | Campaign stage or goal focused on conversion, purchase, or transaction.                                                 |
|  23 | **Preferred rates**                             | Pre-negotiated or discounted pricing, often tied to volume, annual commitments, or publisher relationships.             |
|  24 | **Programmatic**                                | Automated, data-driven buying and selling of ad inventory, often optimized in real time.                                |
|  25 | **Rate card**                                   | Publisher price list based on ad size, placement, platform, format, creative type, or package.                          |
|  26 | **Request for Proposal (RFP)**                  | Buyer asks publishers or vendors to submit a proposal or bid for a campaign.                                            |
|  27 | **Rich media**                                  | Interactive or enhanced ad creative, such as expandable, video, carousel, or interactive units.                         |
|  28 | **RODI: Return on Digital Investment**          | Return metric comparing campaign value or revenue against digital media investment.                                     |
|  29 | **Real-Time Bidding (RTB)**                     | Auction-based buying where impressions are bid on in real time.                                                         |
|  30 | **Run of Site (ROS)**                           | Ad can run broadly across a single site, often without page-level targeting.                                            |
|  31 | **Run of Network (RON)**                        | Ad can run broadly across all or many sites and apps in an ad network.                                                  |
|  32 | **Self-serve media**                            | Advertiser directly creates and buys ads through a platform UI; common in search and social text ads.                   |
|  33 | **Site map**                                    | List or structure of site content; helps users and bots understand site organization.                                   |
|  34 | **Sponsorship**                                 | Premium association with content or placement; often buys visibility, position, and category coverage.                  |
|  35 | **Splash page**                                 | Entry or pre-homepage page, sometimes used for a prominent ad or takeover.                                              |
|  36 | **Targeted Rating Point (TRP)**                 | Rating points within the target audience only; asks, “what percentage of my target saw the ad?”                         |
|  37 | **Upfront commitments**                         | Media-buying commitments made ahead of time, often for reserved inventory or airtime.                                   |

## 4. Measurement Formulas

### Gross Rating Point, or GRP
$$
GRP = Reach \times Frequency
$$

GRP measures broad population exposure. It is commonly used in media planning to understand total advertising pressure across a general audience.

### Targeted Rating Point, or TRP

```text
TRP = Target Reach × Frequency
```

TRP is similar to GRP, but it focuses only on the target audience. It is more useful when the campaign cares about a specific demographic or behavioral segment.

### On-Target Percentage, or OTP

```text
OTP = On-target impressions / Total impressions
```

OTP measures delivery quality. A higher OTP means a larger share of impressions reached the intended audience.

### Video Completion Rate, or VCR

```text
VCR = Completed video views / Video starts
```

VCR measures video completion quality. It is commonly used for video and streaming ad campaigns.

## 6. Interview-Ready Takeaway

In advertising, most terms fall into a pipeline: define the audience, collect and activate data, buy inventory, serve the creative experience, and measure whether the right audience saw the ad and whether the spend created value.
