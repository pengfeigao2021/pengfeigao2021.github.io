In the old story, a donkey falls into a well. When people throw dirt into the well, the donkey does not allow itself to be buried. Instead, it shakes off each shovelful of dirt, steps on it, and gradually climbs higher until it escapes.

In 2026, many people worry that AI will eliminate jobs or make their skills obsolete. That risk is real, but the story offers another perspective. AI is not available only to a small group of experts or large technology companies. Powerful tools, including many open-source models, are increasingly accessible to ordinary people. The same technology that may disrupt your work can also help you work faster, learn new skills, build things that were previously out of reach, and accomplish more with limited time and resources. Instead of allowing AI to bury us, we can learn to step on it. The goal is not to compete against AI alone, but to harness it as a tool—to become more capable, more creative, and more competitive than we were before.
