Softmax turns raw model scores into probabilities. Temperature does not change token order, but it changes how sharp or flat the probability distribution becomes. This is why lower temperature makes generation safer and repetitive, while higher temperature makes generation more diverse and risky.

How to read this

T < 1: sharpen T = 1: original T > 1: flatten
  • Logits are raw scores. They are not probabilities and do not need to sum to 1.
  • Softmax uses relative gaps. Adding the same constant to every logit does not change probabilities.
  • Lower T magnifies gaps. The top token becomes dominant.
  • Higher T shrinks gaps. Lower-ranked tokens get more chance.
  • T does not change ranking. It changes confidence, entropy, and diversity.

Concrete generated-token intuition

Imagine these tokens are possible next characters for a name. A low temperature might keep choosing the safest next letter, producing common-looking names. A high temperature may sample rare letters more often, producing more surprising but sometimes strange names.

Memory hook: Temperature is a confidence knob, not a knowledge knob. It reshapes the probability distribution used for sampling; it does not retrain the model or change the logits themselves.
